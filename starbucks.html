Starbucks Seating App (Feature)
An addition to the Starbucks app that I feel is VERY much needed
Objective Application Section Product Section How It Works
This was a personal project that I worked on out of pure frustration. Since I freelance, I can usually be found in a Starbucks. However, since I also live in NYC, everybody else has the same idea. Starbucks coffee shops are always packed, but you never know just how packed they are. There may be one seat in a corner near an inconspicuous outlet (which is a jackpot), or there may be absolutely no seats at all. Then, when it's 20 degrees outside and you're walking a few blocks to get ready to start working only to find out that there's no available space, it becomes a major inconvenience. No spots means trecking another 5-6 blocks to the next Starbs (which may not sound like a lot, but an inconvenience is still an inconvenience). Thus, I decided to create a concept for a new Starbucks feature that enables users to tell what their local coffee shops look like before going there.

The feature heavily depends on the user's engagement in order to help other users. Conceptually, there will be a small switch installed into the shared and individual tables. When a customer sits down, they can flip this switch on, which will prompt the app to update and fill in a seat on the seating chart. When the customer is finished up, they switch off, and the seat becomes available on the app.


Objective:
Create a new app feature on the Starbucks application that allows users to view available seats at local coffee shops before actually arriving to the location.

Application Section



Design Process
There wasn't much wireframing for this project seeing as the app is already created and this would just be an additional feature. So I began by using screenshots from the Starbucks app and adjusting them to include this new functionality.



Working Prototype/App Interaction
After adjusting the screenshots from the app, I created a new page that included the seating chart for a Starbucks in Brookyln. From here, I created a prototype for the interaction of the application.




Product Section



The second part of the design includes product design and user engagement. In order for the app to work, customers need to actively engage with it in order to keep it updated in real-time.


The Product
The most important part of this concept is the on-off switch installed in every table top. The customer will see one of these switches wherever they want to sit down. It is their responsibility to switch it to "seated" when occupied and "open" when they are leaving.



This is an example of what a shared table might look like at a Starbucks. There will be a switch at every individual seat.
